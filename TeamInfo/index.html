Roles

  • Chief Building Officer (CBO)
    1. Resposible for answering questions about Robot Design, Assembly
    2. Study the missions thoroughly, and understand the tasks
    3. Assign tasks to sub teams (2-people x 4 sub-teams), with adults' help
    4. Oversee the status of each sub-team's work
    5. Work with CIO to gather information and learn from experience

  • Chief Communication Officer (CCO)
    1. Resposible for handling all external communication matters
    2. Check FLL website about updates and tournament information, inform the whole team weekly
    3. Works with CRO to arrange interviews with experts: e.g., Environment Researcher, Department of Environment Protection, Sanitation worker, and etc.
    4. Plan and arrange field trip to waste management related plant/institute/office/lab
    5. Work with CVO to arrange team building event

  • Chief Information Officer (CIO)
    1. Responsible for collecting team information, project experience and references
    2. Work with CBO, CCO, talk to team members, and collect and organize Robot design documents
    3. Document the successful experience as well as failed lessons
    4. Work with CRO and CVO to write down research and Core Value related experience
    5. Design and maintain team website

  • Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)
    1. Resposible for marketing the whole team, our project and the products, including the Robot
    2. Work on the final Poster to present the Robot mechanical design, programming, and strategy, with CIO's help
    3. With on the final Poster for Project presentation, with CRO's help
    4. Plan, and coordinate the rehearsing the team for final presentation
    5. Work with CVO to finalize the team T-Shirt and make purchase order with Treasury's help

  • Chief Operation Officer (COO)
    1. Responsible for overall progress of team, or assign team member to report status
    2. Plan, follow up and document milestones for the whole season
    3. Talk to other officers to collect project progress, problems, and find solutions
    4. Call for ideas, meetings and plan for field trips
    5. At each meeting, call to gather for "Beginning Talk" and "Ending Talk": assign one person at a time to host the meeting. (The goal is for each team member to have the opportunity to host.)

  • Chief Programming Officer (CPO)
    1. Resposible for answering questions about Software Program to control the Robot
    2. Study the missions thoroughly, and understand the Software rquirements for the tasks
    3. Work with CBO to make the hardware and software work together
    4. Recruit subteam to design, code, and test the programs (could be the same subteam assigned by CBO)
    5. Document the design, the epic fails, and share the knowledge with whole team, working with CIO

  • Chief Research Officer (CRO)
    1. Resposible for answering all Trash Trek research related questions
    2. Gather background information, call for ideas of research subjects, assign research tasks to sub-teams
    3. Analyze and document the details on existing solutions and limitations for the identified problem.
    4. Decide what the action plan is, assign to subteams, and follow up with progress
    5. Gather experience and lessons learned, write research paper, prepare poster and call for presentation rehearsal

  • Chief Value Officer (CVO)
    1. Resposible for answering all Core Value related questions
    2. Call for Team name, logo, ritual, and T-shirt design, make final decision on what to use
    3. Document building experience from core value perspective, and share lessons with team
    4. Observe and promote friendly cooperation and graceful competition
    5. When conflict happens, help find a resolution, help other team members understand compromise and self-discipline
