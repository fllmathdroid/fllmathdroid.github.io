Matthew ~ Slide: What is TrashIndex--Located: In the MIDDLE SECTION (first person talking) == Transitioning Title: Why TrashIndex

    
What you're going to say: TrashIndex is a common measurement to indicate the total amount of trash generated by the production, consumption, and disposal of a product. It measures how much burden a product is adding to our environment. 

    
Andrew ~ Slide: Why TrashIndex--Located: In the MIDDLE SECTION (second person talking) == Transitioning Title: Current Problems

    
What you're going to say: TrashIndex provides a guideline for the consumers to be environmental conscious when they make purchase decisions.
    For instance, a salad packaged in a paper box will have a very low TrashIndex. On the contrary, a salad bowl in a plastic container will have a higher TrashIndex. 
    If there are more environmental friendly products, there will be less trash to decompose on the earth. 

    
Ryan ~ Slide: Current Problems--Located: In the LEFT SECTION (third person talking) == Transitioning Slide: How do poeple access TrashIndex

    
What you're going to say: Trash is becoming a larger and larger problem around the world. As human population grows, so does our waste. Most of the trash ends up in landfills. The products that include dangerous and harmful chemicals will cause pollution. Also, these products will take longer to decompose. 
    Consumers will buy products with lower TrashIndex. Their decisions will help industries make more environment-friendly products.

    
Michael ~ Slide: How Do People Access TrashIndex--Located: In the RIGHT SECTION (fourth person talking) == Transitioning Slide: Leaf Calculator

    
What you're going to say: First of all, our mobile application scans the UPC code of an item, such as a juice bottle or a luxury handbag. 
    Then from the UPC code, our app will detect what materials the product is made up of. After running the calculation, the app will generate a number. A high number indicates materials will take longer to degrade and recyclability is low. A low index indicates the product is earth friendly.
